# MindBridge — 3-Minute Demo & Pitch Script

*Total run time: ~3:00. Rehearse the voice demo beforehand and have a fallback recording ready in case of network issues.*

---

## Beat 1 — Hook & Problem (0:00–0:40)

> "Raise your hand if you'd call a doctor at 3 a.m. because you couldn't stop shaking. Now imagine you were also trained your whole career to never admit weakness. That's the corner a lot of veterans are backed into."

- About **1 in 11 U.S. veterans** will live with **PTSD** — higher than the civilian rate.
- The hard part isn't just the symptoms. It's the **reluctance to ask for help**: stigma, self-reliance, and waitlists.
- **The neuroscience credibility angle:** PTSD isn't weakness — it's a nervous system stuck in threat mode. The **amygdala** (the brain's smoke alarm) gets hyperactive, while the **prefrontal cortex** that normally says "you're safe now" goes quiet. That's *why* a flashback feels like a real emergency — and it's also why **paced breathing and grounding work**: they re-engage the calming (parasympathetic) system and help the thinking brain come back online.

> "MindBridge is built on that one insight: you can't reason someone out of a threat response — but you can help them get grounded, and then get them to a human."

---

## Beat 2 — Live Voice Demo (0:40–1:40)

> "MindBridge isn't an app you type into. You call it and you talk. Watch."

**[Speak out loud to the interface — plain, tired tone:]**
> "Hey… I can't sleep again. Keep hearing the convoy. I don't even know why I'm talking to a computer about this."

**Let MindBridge respond live.** Point out, in real time, what it does:
- **Names it's an AI** honestly — no pretending to be a therapist.
- **Validates** in plain talk: *"That sound following you home is real, and you're not broken for hearing it."*
- **Offers a concrete grounding step** — e.g., a slow 4-in / 6-out breath, or a 5-senses check — and does it *with* the user.
- **Respects military culture** — no clinical lecture, no "how does that make you feel."

> "Notice what it did NOT do: it didn't diagnose me, didn't prescribe anything, didn't tell me I have a disorder. It's a battle-buddy, not a clipboard."

---

## Beat 3 — The Crisis-Safety Moment (Emotional Peak) (1:40–2:20)

> "But here's the moment that matters most. What happens when talking isn't enough?"

**[Speak a line that signals real risk:]**
> "Honestly, some nights I think everyone would be better off without me."

**Let the escalation fire live.** The tone shifts — calm, direct, human:
- MindBridge **detects the crisis language** and **immediately, gently pivots**.
- It **does not try to counsel or talk the veteran down alone.** It says, plainly: *"I hear you, and I'm not going anywhere — but this is bigger than me, and you deserve a real person right now."*
- It surfaces the **Veterans Crisis Line: dial 988, then press 1** (or text 838255) — and offers to stay on the line while they reach out.

> "This is the whole design philosophy in one moment: MindBridge knows exactly what it is — and what it isn't. Its proudest feature is handing you off to a human."

*(Pause. Let it land.)*

---

## Beat 4 — How We Hit Each Prize Track (2:20–2:45)

Rapid-fire, one line each:

- **Best Use of Gradient AI** — The whole companion runs on **DigitalOcean Gradient serverless inference**, with an **agent + knowledge-base RAG** pipeline that grounds every response in vetted, veteran-specific content instead of hallucinating.
- **Best Use of Data** — Coping ideas and referrals are pulled from **real DataSF veteran resource data**, so "get help" means *actual* local services, not a generic hotline.
- **Best UI/UX** — There is no cluttered screen. The interface is a **calm voice call** — the lowest-friction way for someone in distress to reach out. Design *is* the accessibility.
- **Best Beginner** — Built by a hackathon-first team, start to finish, on managed DigitalOcean infrastructure.

---

## Beat 5 — Closing Impact & Ask (2:45–3:00)

> "The distance between 'I'm not okay' and 'I called someone' is where we lose veterans. MindBridge exists to shorten that distance to a single phone call — one that answers at 3 a.m., never judges, and always knows when to get a human on the line."

> "We're not trying to replace therapists or crisis counselors. We're trying to be the steady voice that gets one more veteran to reach them. Thanks — we'd love your vote and your feedback."

*(End. Have SAFETY.md open in a tab in case judges ask about crisis handling — lead them there proactively; it's a strength.)*
